About WSIL?

When Should I Leave (WSIL?) is a web tool created to help ease commuter traffic, geolocate dense hotspots and help future planning. Whether it be to avoid rush hour or to head towards the hottest new bar in town, WSIL? helps give users vital decision-making information.
Created by a team of four hackers, WSIL? specifically targets the problems caused by peak hour congestion and concentrated pedestrian flow. Often, accidents occur during rush hours when public areas are denser due to greater foot traffic[1], especially in cities where a significant percentage of the population utilises public transport [2].
As an idea, WSIL? is scalable. When implemented with working city Smart-sensors, WSIL? would be better able to provide traffic flow maps in real-time, and adjust predictions by taking into account weather conditions, seasonal tourism, and organised events. It is hoped that with the widespread use of WSIL? overground pedestrian density would be better moderated, decreasing the likelihood of accidents whilst allowing users to better optimise their time.
